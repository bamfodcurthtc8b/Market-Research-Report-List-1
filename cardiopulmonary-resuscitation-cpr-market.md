<p><p>The CPR market can be categorized into battery-driven and pneumatic-driven devices. Battery-driven CPR equipment relies on rechargeable or disposable batteries, providing portability and ease of use during emergencies, often incorporating features like automated monitoring. In contrast, pneumatic-driven devices utilize compressed air to generate pressure for chest compressions, delivering consistent force while minimizing operator fatigue. Both types aim to enhance the effectiveness of cardiopulmonary resuscitation, ensuring timely assistance during cardiac arrest scenarios in various settings.</p></p>
